Case Study · 01

Lumen — Banking, Reimagined.

A ground-up redesign of a regional bank's mobile app — built around clarity, trust, and the quiet confidence of doing one thing well.

Role

Lead UI/UX Designer

Year

2025

Tools

Figma, Principle, Maze

Client

Lumen Financial

Live Site → View Prototype →

01 · The Challenge

What problem we solved.

Lumen had a 2.1-star App Store rating and a churn rate climbing six percent quarter-over-quarter. The existing app was technically functional — but users described it as "overwhelming," "corporate," and "hard to trust."

Every common task — transferring money, checking a balance, freezing a card — took four or more taps and required mental gymnastics with banking jargon. The product team had been adding features for two years. The app needed fewer features, not more.

Our challenge wasn't to add. It was to strip down to what mattered, and make every interaction feel intentional.

02 · Process

How we got there.

We started with what wasn't working. Data showed 73% of session time was spent on three tasks — yet those tasks were two screens deep. The visual design was downstream of the structural problem.

From there, we ran twelve rounds of wireframes before touching any visual design. Every screen had to justify its existence. We killed the side menu. We collapsed four tab sections into three. We rewrote every label to use plain English — "Move money" instead of "Initiate transfer."

Two rounds of unmoderated usability testing on Maze (n=24 each) helped us catch friction we'd missed. By the time we showed the team a prototype, we already knew it worked.

03 · The Solution

The final design.

The final design centers on a single large action card that always shows what's most relevant — a recent transfer to repeat, an upcoming bill, your latest spending insight. Tap it, and you're already mid-flow.

The visual system uses a single accent color, a typography pair that rewards skimming over reading, and motion that confirms every action without celebrating it. Trust, not flash.

04 · Outcome

Impact & learnings.

In the three months after launch, Lumen's App Store rating rose from 2.1 to 4.6. Task completion time dropped 38%. Customer support tickets related to the app fell by half.

But the metric I think about most is qualitative — three different users wrote unsolicited five-star reviews saying the app finally "felt like it respects my time."

What I learned: design constraints aren't enemies; they're collaborators. Every "no" we said to a feature made the "yes" to a clearer flow possible. I bring that discipline to every project now.
