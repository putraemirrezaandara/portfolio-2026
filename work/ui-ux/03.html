01 · The Challenge

What problem we solved.

Describe the problem this project addressed. Who was the user? What were their pain points? What constraints did the project have (time, scope, technical limitations)?

A good challenge section frames the stakes — why this work matters, and what makes it harder than it looks at first glance.

02 · Process

How we got there.

Walk through your design process. What research did you do? What were the early ideas? Where did you pivot, and why?

Specifics make this section credible: user interview count, number of iterations, key design decisions and the reasoning behind them.

03 · The Solution

The final design.

Present the final product. Highlight key screens, the visual system, and the moments you're most proud of. Use captions on screens to point out specific design choices.

04 · Outcome

Impact & learnings.

What changed because of this work? Use real numbers if you have them (conversion lift, task time reduction, NPS, downloads). If not, talk about qualitative wins — user quotes, stakeholder feedback, what you learned.

End on a reflection — what you'd do differently next time, what surprised you, what this taught you about your craft.
