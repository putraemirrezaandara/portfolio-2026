Case Study · 01

Sohnne — Iconic Design, Within Reach.

Redesigning the e-commerce experience for a mid-century modern furniture brand built on bringing iconic designs to market at a third of the originals' price.

Role

UI/UX Designer

Year

2024

Tools

Figma, Maze, Hotjar

Client

Sohnne Furniture

Visit Sohnne → View Prototype →

01 · The Challenge

What problem we solved.

Sohnne sells $849–$4,199 furniture in a category dominated by two extremes: cheap mass-market knockoffs on one end, $10K+ originals on the other. The brand sits squarely in the middle with a clear proposition — 1:1 replicas of iconic mid-century designs at roughly a third of the originals' price — but the existing site didn't communicate that.

Visitors either dismissed it as "just another replica brand" or couldn't tell how it differed from generic furniture marketplaces. Bounce rate on product pages was high, and conversion sat well below e-commerce benchmarks for the price tier.

The challenge: build a shopping experience where the value proposition isn't just stated — it's demonstrated, on every page, in a way that builds trust over a single session.

02 · Process

How we got there.

Research surfaced two non-negotiable user needs. Buyers wanted to see the craftsmanship difference, not just read about it. And they needed comparison context — how does Sohnne stack up against the original, and against the cheap alternatives?

This shaped the entire structural approach: a comparison-first design language. Side-by-side specs, materials, and dimensions across Sohnne / Original / Mass-Market — not in a buried FAQ, but on the home page and on every product page.

Mobile-first was non-negotiable. 68% of furniture browsing happens on phones, even though purchases skew desktop. Every key flow was prototyped for thumb-reach first. Hotjar recordings of the legacy mobile experience showed where rage-clicks were happening — and most of them were on the filter and quick-view interactions, which we rebuilt from scratch.

03 · The Solution

The final design.

The redesigned homepage opens with a three-column value comparison table — Sohnne vs. the original vs. mass-market. It's the first thing visitors see, and it does the persuasion work before any product photography.

Product pages follow the same logic: hero shot of the piece, then immediately a comparison panel showing what makes Sohnne's version functionally equivalent to the original. The visual system is restrained — generous whitespace, large typography, a single accent — so the product photography can do the heavy lifting.

The Discover hub turns inspirational interior shots into shoppable layouts, surfacing related Sohnne pieces with a single tap. The Custom and Trade portals route B2B inquiries away from the main flow without cluttering the consumer experience.

04 · Outcome

Impact & learnings.

In the months following launch (replace these placeholders with your real numbers):

Home-page bounce dropped from [XX]% to [XX]%. Product-page-to-cart conversion lifted by [XX]%. Mobile session duration increased [XX]%. Trade portal inquiries grew [X]× month-over-month.

But the qualitative win mattered more: customer-support tickets that used to start with "is this real wood?" or "are these the originals?" largely disappeared. The site now answers those questions before they're asked.

What I learned: in categories where trust is the bottleneck, explaining your value is design work, not copy work. The comparison table did more for conversion than any photoshoot.
